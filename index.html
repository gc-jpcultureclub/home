Winter Tradition

"Kakizome" and "Mamemaki"

Kakizome is a traditional event of writing our New Year's goals or wishes in Japanese calligraphy.

Mamemaki is also a tradition for wishing everyone health and happiness. "Mame" means beans, "maki" means scattering away and we throw beans saying "Devils out! Happiness in!" Also, people eat the same numbers of beans as their age.

Tanabata Decoration

Celebrate the Tanabata and wish yourself luck!

Tanabata festival is an annual event celebrated on July 7th and based on the Chinese myth story featuring two stars, Vega and Altair. It is believed your wishes will come true if you write it down on paper and hang it on a bamboo tree. We will put a small tree object for students to hang papers. Join us to make your wish come true!

Japanese Festival

You can try festival games and foods!

The origin of our festivals is a ceremony for praying to gods and thanking deities in nature. This concept came from the Japanese religion "Shinto" which believes that deities are everywhere in nature. Even though its religious origin is not recognized very much today, people still enjoy game stands, food, fireworks and wearing a "yukata" which is a casual kimono. We will design mini-games and offer some festival food.

Traditional Games

Games we all played in our childhood

Most of the traditional games were brought or developed and became popular during the Edo period (around early 17th to late 19th century). It is said that there was one game the government had banned because of it being too popular at the period. You can try “Origami", "Kendama”, spinning top, etc at our event.
